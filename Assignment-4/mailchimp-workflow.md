## 🎯 Email Segmentation Strategy

### Segments to Create in Mailchimp:

1. **New Subscribers** (0-7 days)
   - Welcome series
   - First-time buyer discount

2. **Engaged Non-Buyers** (7-30 days)
   - Product showcases
   - Testimonials
   - Design inspiration

3. **First-Time Buyers** (made 1 purchase)
   - Thank you emails
   - Review requests
   - Cross-sell products

4. **Repeat Customers** (2+ purchases)
   - VIP treatment
   - Early access to sales
   - Loyalty rewards

5. **High-Value Customers** ($200+ lifetime value)
   - Exclusive offers
   - Beta features
   - Personal account manager

6. **Inactive Subscribers** (60+ days no engagement)
   - Re-engagement campaigns
   - Survey for feedback
   - Last-chance offers

7. **Cart Abandoners**
   - Reminder emails
   - Discount incentives
   - Urgency messaging

## ✅ Email Best Practices

### Subject Lines:
- Keep under 50 characters
- Use emojis strategically (1-2 max)
- Personalize with first name
- Create urgency or curiosity
- Avoid spam trigger words

**Good Examples:**
- "Your 15% OFF code expires tonight! 🎁"
- "[Name], your custom design is ready 🎨"
- "New arrivals you'll love (especially #3)"

**Avoid:**
- "FREE FREE FREE!!!" (too spammy)
- All caps subjects
- Excessive punctuation!!!
- Misleading claims

### Email Design:
- Mobile-first approach (60% open on mobile)
- Single column layout
- Clear CTA buttons
- Images with alt text
- Maximum 600px width
- Fast loading time

### Content:
- Personalize with first name
- Keep copy concise and scannable
- One primary CTA per email
- Include social proof
- Clear value proposition
- Strong call-to-action

### Timing:
- **Best days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times**: 10 AM, 2 PM, 8 PM (test your audience)
- **Avoid**: Early mornings, late nights, weekends (except promotions)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 emails per week (max 4 for promotions)

## 🧪 A/B Testing Strategy

### Elements to Test:

1. **Subject Lines**
   - Length (short vs. long)
   - Emoji usage
   - Personalization
   - Urgency vs. curiosity

2. **Send Times**
   - Morning vs. afternoon
   - Weekday vs. weekend
   - Different time zones

3. **Content**
   - Image vs. text heavy
   - Long vs. short copy
   - Single vs. multiple products

4. **CTAs**
   - Button text ("Shop Now" vs. "Get Started")
   - Button color
   - Placement
   - Number of CTAs

5. **Sender Name**
   - Company name
   - Personal name
   - "Team" designation

### Testing Process:
1. Test one element at a time
2. Use 20% of list for A/B test
3. Send winner to remaining 80%
4. Document all results
5. Implement learnings
